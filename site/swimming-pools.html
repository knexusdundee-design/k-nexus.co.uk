Swimming & Aquatics

K-NEXUS Community Sanctuary Hub — Dundee

🏊 K-NEXUS Pools Wing

A proper safe, community-owned aquatic facility — not Olympia. No crumbling tiles, no random closures. Built for real people: lap swimmers, families, elderly users, hydrotherapy patients, and those who just want a hot tub without a hotel bill. Full ventilation, zero-downtime target, smooth surfaces throughout.

🏅 25m Lap Pool

  • Semi-Olympic — 25 metres, 6 lanes
  • Lane ropes with clearly marked speed zones
  • Touchscreen lane booking at poolside
  • NHS Tayside aquatic therapy sessions
  • Masters swimming club — weekly sessions
  • Beginner lessons — all ages

🌊 Leisure Pool & Slides

  • Safe leisure pool — shallow entry, no sharp edges
  • Water slides — enclosed, smooth, rounded exits
  • Kids splash zone with toys — gated, supervised
  • Floats & inflatables available
  • Sensory swim sessions — reduced noise/light

♨ Whirlpool & Hot Tub

Full whirlpool and hot tub suite. Smooth surfaces, rounded corners, non-slip floor — designed for safe use by elderly members, NHS hydrotherapy, and post-exercise recovery.

  • Maintained at therapeutic temperature
  • Accessible entry — steps + hoist available
  • Quieter sessions: Mon–Fri mornings

🛟 Merry Maiden Rentals

Daily professional floatation rings, pool noodles, and therapy floats available for hire. Bring your own or rent at the desk — member card discount.

  • Floatation tanks — appointment booking
  • Buoyancy aids for all abilities
  • Pool noodles, kickboards, pull buoys

🤖 AI Safety — Rift Drown Watch

Rift AI monitors all pool surfaces via overhead sensors. Drown detection, occupancy tracking, and unauthorised zone alerts fire in real-time to staff terminals. No system replaces lifeguards — Rift supports them.

Real-time sensor monitoring Drown detection AI Occupancy heatmap Lifeguard-first policy

💰 Funding — sportscotland Sport Facilities Fund

K-NEXUS is actively applying for sportscotland capital funding (up to £250,000) to build and fit this wing. Application deadlines: 1 April 2026 and 1 September 2026. Community ownership is a core eligibility criterion — CIC structure gives us a strong case.
